# Hiring Narrative

## Goal

Create a consistent story for recruiters, hiring managers, design leads, and interview panels.

## Core Narrative

Cello is a product designer with technical fluency. He is strongest where product teams need clear UX thinking, realistic prototyping, design-system judgment, and better communication between design and engineering.

## Interview Framing

Lead with:

- Product problems solved
- Users and workflows clarified
- Systems or patterns designed
- Prototype decisions that reduced ambiguity
- Tradeoffs between design intent and implementation reality
- Outcomes or observable improvements

## Differentiator

The differentiator is not "designer who codes" as a novelty. It is a designer who uses technical understanding to make product decisions clearer, prototypes more realistic, and collaboration more precise.

## Proof Points

- Ghosted shows AI-assisted workflow design, local-first constraints, and keyboard-first product thinking.
- Ad Asset Directory shows internal tool design, information architecture, admin workflows, and measurable time savings.
- Ascendant Traders shows product-system thinking across acquisition, runtime state, publishing, diagnostics, and trust.
- VegAvengers shows mission-driven discovery, flexible search, and responsive interaction design.
- The planned Figma/Payload prototype will become the flagship design-dev collaboration case study.

## Resume And LinkedIn Alignment

Use the same language across the website, resume, GitHub, LinkedIn, and video content:

- Product Designer
- Technical prototyping
- Design systems
- Frontend-capable UX
- Design-dev collaboration
- Product storytelling
