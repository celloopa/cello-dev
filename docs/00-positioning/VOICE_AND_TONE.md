# Voice And Tone

## Goal

Keep the portfolio confident, specific, and product-focused without sounding inflated or overly technical.

## Voice Principles

- Clear before clever
- Specific before broad
- Human before jargon
- Product outcomes before tool lists
- Technical depth in service of design clarity

## Preferred Language

Use:

- Product design
- Product systems
- Technical prototyping
- Design systems
- Implementation intent
- Buildable UX
- Workflow clarity
- Cross-functional collaboration
- Product storytelling

## Avoided Language

Avoid:

- "I do everything"
- "Jack of all trades"
- Generic "creative technologist" as the main identity
- Long unframed tool lists
- Developer-first descriptions when the role target is product design

## Tone Examples

Good:

> I design product experiences and build realistic prototypes that help teams move from ambiguity to buildable solutions.

Too broad:

> I am a designer, developer, photographer, marketer, and creative problem solver.

Good:

> I use code to test interaction behavior, data constraints, and edge cases before teams commit to a build.

Too developer-first:

> I build full-stack applications with modern frameworks and APIs.
