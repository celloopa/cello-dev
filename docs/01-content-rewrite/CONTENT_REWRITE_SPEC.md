# Content Rewrite Spec

## Objective

Update the current portfolio website content before any major backend or CMS migration.

The goal is to make the existing site immediately read as a focused Product Designer portfolio.

Do not refactor the backend. Do not migrate to Payload yet. Do not rebuild the whole site architecture unless a small route or content change is clearly low-risk.

## Core Positioning

Cello is a Product Designer focused on design systems, technical prototyping, and design-dev collaboration.

He bridges design and engineering through thoughtful prototypes, systems thinking, and clear product storytelling.

## What This Phase Should Accomplish

The site should quickly answer:

1. What role is Cello targeting?
2. What type of product designer is he?
3. What is his strongest differentiator?
4. What work should people look at first?
5. Why does his technical background matter for product design?

## Primary Message

Cello designs product experiences and builds realistic prototypes that help designers, developers, and stakeholders move from ambiguity to buildable solutions.

## Value Cards

### Product Systems

I design scalable systems, workflows, and reusable patterns that help teams create more consistent product experiences.

### Technical Prototyping

I use tools like Figma, React, TypeScript, Payload, Go, Python, and CMS platforms to make product ideas easier to test and explain.

### Design-Dev Collaboration

I help teams clarify requirements, edge cases, interaction behavior, content rules, and implementation tradeoffs before build.

## Featured Work Order

1. Figma/Payload Feature Prototype
2. Design System / Brand Operations
3. Automation / Product Workflows
4. Ghosted or technical product artifact
5. Visual Archive

## Avoid

Avoid:

- Generic "designer and developer" as the main identity
- Long tool lists without outcomes
- Making WordPress the central positioning
- Making the site sound like a pure software engineering portfolio
- Mixing visual archive work equally with product design case studies
- Over-explaining biography before showing proof
