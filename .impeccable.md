## Design Context

### Users
This site is a personal portfolio for Marcelo Rondon, a front-end developer and designer. The primary audience is hiring managers evaluating whether to interview or hire him, with collaborators, freelance clients, and peers as secondary audiences. Visitors are likely browsing quickly across desktop and mobile and need to understand credibility, craft, range, and contact paths without friction.

### Brand Personality
The brand personality should feel distinctive, earthy, and effortless. It should present Marcelo as someone who bridges design systems, accessibility, front-end engineering, and visual storytelling with confidence and originality, without feeling overworked or performative.

### Aesthetic Direction
The repo already signals a Saul Bass-inspired direction with high contrast, geometric accents, assertive typography, and motion that feels deliberate instead of ornamental. The current implementation supports both light and dark themes, with deep green, warm earth, and bright green accents. Future design work should treat dark mode as the first-class experience while preserving light mode as an intentional, fully supported switch. The overall feel should stay authored and poster-like while avoiding template portfolio patterns.

### Design Principles
1. Optimize for hiring-manager decisions: every key page should help someone quickly understand fit, strengths, and professionalism.
2. Lead with authorship: every major page should feel intentionally composed, not assembled from stock portfolio sections.
3. Prove range quickly: show engineering rigor and visual taste together instead of separating them into disconnected modes.
4. Keep motion purposeful: animation should reinforce hierarchy, pacing, and personality without obscuring content.
5. Preserve accessibility as a design quality: strong contrast, keyboard support, reduced-motion care, and readable layouts should be baseline expectations.

### Open Assumptions
- The Saul Bass-inspired note in the README remains a useful stylistic reference, but the exact list of visual references and anti-references is still unspecified.
